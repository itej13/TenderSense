# Ports & Waterways Sector — Consultancy Tender Research & Evaluation Framework

**Sector:** `ports` — Ports & Waterways
**Prepared:** 2026-07-18
**Purpose:** Feed the ports-sector module of a webapp that helps consultancy firms self-assess against Indian government ports / shipping / inland-waterways consultancy tenders (feasibility/TEFR, DPR, detailed engineering, master planning, PMC/PMU, transaction advisory for PPP terminals, EIA/CRZ studies, and dredging/hydrographic-survey supervision).

---

## 1. Landscape Overview

India's maritime capital programme is large and consultancy-intensive, and is driven by three overlapping policy vehicles:

- **Sagarmala** (MoPSW) — the flagship port-led development programme. It generates most of the sector's **feasibility (TEFR)**, **master-plan** and **DPR** consultancy demand, both directly (Sagarmala / Sagarmala Development Company) and through the major ports. The Sagarmala National Perspective Plan portal publishes completed TEFRs for new/expanded ports (Vadhavan, Sagar Island, Dugarajapatnam, Colachel, Sirkazhi, Belekeri) and the Chennai Port Master Plan — each the output of a consultancy engagement.
- **Maritime India Vision 2030 / Amrit Kaal Vision 2047 & the National Shipbuilding Mission** — push capacity augmentation, PPP terminals, and a new wave of **shipbuilding/ship-repair clusters** (Tuticorin, Dugarajapatnam, Cuddalore), each needing feasibility, DPR and PMU consultancies.
- **Jal Marg Vikas Project / IWAI (inland waterways)** — World Bank-financed capacity augmentation of National Waterway-1 spawned a full suite of consultancies (detailed feasibility & engineering, ESIA/EMP/RAP, IWT sector strategy, ferry-service definition studies, asset management and technical support services), all under QCBS.

Alongside these, the **major port authorities** (JNPA, Deendayal/Kandla, Paradip, Visakhapatnam, Chennai, V.O. Chidambaranar, Cochin, Mormugao, New Mangalore, Syama Prasad Mookerjee/Kolkata, Kamarajar) and **state maritime boards** run their own consultancy procurements — transaction advisory for PPP/DBFOT berths, EIA/CRZ for capital dredging, SEZ/hinterland feasibility, and DPRs for expansion.

**Consultant selection is overwhelmingly by QCBS.** The legal/procedural backbone is **GFR 2017 (Rules 192/193)** and the **MoF Manual for Procurement of Consultancy & Other Services, 2022**. GFR caps the technical (non-financial) weight at **80%**, so the highest ratio in practice is **80:20**; **70:30** is also common. Externally-financed IWAI work follows the equivalent World Bank QCBS process. Minimum qualifying technical scores cluster at **60%** for many port RFPs and **70-75%** for larger or multilateral-financed assignments.

A recurring eligibility feature specific to this sector is **empanelment**: several major-port advisory RFPs are restricted to firms on the **Indian Ports Association (IPA)** panel, the **NICSI Tier-I** list, or (for PPP) DEA transaction-advisor-style panels.

## 3. Tender-by-Tender Notes (14 tenders)

### ports-001 — VOCPA (Tuticorin): Basic Engineering & Depth Feasibility Study, Shipbuilding Cluster — *live-tender*  ⭐ most detailed
- **Method:** QCBS; **min technical 60/100**. Application window 16.03.2026–15.04.2026.
- **Eligibility gates:** avg annual turnover ≥ **Rs 5 Cr** (3 FYs); positive net worth; not blacklisted. **Technical:** 1 port project (Feasibility/DPR/Detailed Engineering) for a shipbuilding yard ≥ **Rs 1000 Cr**, **or** two port projects each ≥ **Rs 700 Cr**, in last 7 yrs. "Eligible Assignment" = port-facilities project of capital cost ≥ **Rs 500 Cr**.
- **Technical scoring (100):** applicant experience **30** (70% for one eligible assignment, +15% each additional, cap 30); methodology & work plan **20** (Understanding of TOR 20% / Methodology 50% / Work plan 30%); **key personnel 40** (Team Leader 15; Civil 5–8; Mechanical 4–5; Environmental 4; Traffic 4; Legal 4); presentation **10**.
- **Knock-out rule:** each key person must score ≥ 60%; proposal rejected if **Team Leader < 60%** or **any two other key experts < 60%**. This RFP is the quantitative backbone of the synthesised framework.

## 4. Evaluation Criteria Synthesis

Across the sample, consultant evaluation is consistently a **two-envelope QCBS** process: mandatory eligibility → 100-point technical score → financial opening only for bids above the minimum technical threshold. The **VOCPA Tuticorin RFP (ports-001)** provides the clearest fully-quantified matrix and is used to anchor the framework, cross-checked against the transaction-advisory RFPs (Paradip, Chennai), the PMU/EoI (VPA), and the IWAI/World Bank assignments.

**Five criteria families** (synthesised weights, summing to 100):

| # | Criterion | Typical weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Firm's relevant port/maritime experience | **30%** | Counted as "Eligible Assignments" above a capital-cost threshold (Rs 500 / 700 / 1000 Cr); 70% for the first, +15% each additional. |
| 2 | Financial strength & standing (turnover / net worth / empanelment) | **10%** | Mainly a hard gate (e.g. VOCPA ≥ Rs 5 Cr avg turnover, positive net worth); IPA / NICSI-Tier-I / DEA-panel empanelment can be mandatory. |
| 3 | Key personnel (CVs by role) | **35%** | Largest scored block. Team Leader most weighted + knock-out rule (TL < 60% or two experts < 60% ⇒ rejection). Roles: Port Planner/TL, Marine-Structures Engineer, Mechanical/Equipment, Dredging/Hydrographic, Environmental & CRZ, Traffic/Commercial, Financial/Transaction Advisor, Legal. |
| 4 | Approach, methodology & work plan | **15%** | Understanding of TOR / methodology / activity-wise work plan (VOCPA: 20/50/30 split within the block). |
| 5 | Technical presentation | **10%** | Distinct scored criterion in several major-port RFPs; "team interaction" in advisory RFPs. |

**Dominant method:** QCBS — **80:20** for major-port advisory/feasibility, **70:30** widely used; **typical minimum technical score 60%** (70–75% for larger/multilateral-financed work).

**Practical implication for a bidding firm:** clearing eligibility (turnover, net worth, required empanelment, and at least one qualifying high-value port "Eligible Assignment") is necessary but not sufficient. Competitive scores come from (a) the **depth and value of directly comparable port/maritime references** — greenfield ports, terminals, shipbuilding clusters, dredging and waterway assignments; (b) **fielding all required named experts** above their minimum qualification/experience floors (with a strong Team Leader who has led ≥ 3 eligible port assignments); and (c) a **genuinely project-specific methodology** backed by a confident **presentation** delivered by the actual proposed team. The webapp's input fields quantify exactly these levers (counts and values of similar assignments; Team Leader years and led-assignments; specialist-role coverage; methodology specificity; presentation readiness).

## 5. Policy Document Findings

- **MoF Manual for Procurement of Consultancy & Other Services, 2022** and **GFR 2017 (Rules 192/193)** — establish QCBS as the default for quality-sensitive consultancy, mandate that the RFP fix the minimum qualifying technical score and the technical:financial weights, and cap the non-financial weight at **80%** (hence 80:20 is the observed ceiling). They also codify the Consultancy Evaluation Committee and the combined-score formula.
- **IICA QCBS Illustration** — a clean worked example: technical grading bands, rejection of bids below the qualifying technical score (≥ 70 in the illustration), conversion of price to a financial score, and the 70:30 combined computation. Useful to explain the arithmetic the webapp emulates.
- **Sagarmala National Perspective Plan portal** — confirms the scale of feasibility/master-plan/DPR consultancy demand and lists completed TEFRs (Vadhavan, Sagar Island, Dugarajapatnam, Colachel, Sirkazhi, Belekeri) and the Chennai Port Master Plan as consultancy outputs.
- **World Bank Jal Marg Vikas Procurement Plan** — the authoritative record of IWAI's inland-waterway consultancies on NW-1, with selection method (QCBS), review type and **awarded values**, anchoring the waterways side of the framework.

## 6. Gaps & Caveats

- **Detailed scoring matrices** are only fully public for VOCPA (ports-001) and, partially, for VOCPA's advisory RFPs. For most other live tenders (Paradip, Chennai, Cochin, IPA, SMP, Mormugao, SDCL, VPA) the exact marks-per-criterion sit inside RFP PDFs behind DSC-gated CPP-portal downloads; their `technicalCriteria` are left empty and `minTechnicalScore` null rather than guessed. The synthesised framework generalises the VOCPA matrix and the MoF/GFR/IICA method — appropriate for indicative self-assessment, not a substitute for reading each RFP.
- **Turnover thresholds** vary widely by assignment size and were only firmly captured for VOCPA (≥ Rs 5 Cr). Large multi-year PMC/advisory assignments will set materially higher gates.
- **Some URLs are aggregator/news mirrors** (TenderShark; The Hindu; The Goan) used deliberately because they are stable and verifiable, whereas the underlying CPP-portal links are session-tokened and expire. IWAI entries share one World Bank procurement-plan document (a single verifiable source covering multiple assignments).
- **Not separately tendered here:** JNPA, Deendayal (transaction-advisor tender exists but its RFP body was inaccessible), New Mangalore, Kamarajar, and standalone **dredging-supervision / hydrographic-survey** consultancies — these are represented within the framework's criteria and role list even though a clean, fully-sourced individual RFP was not captured for each.
