# Road & Highways Sector — Consultancy Tender Research & Evaluation Framework

*Generated 2026-07-18. Focus: consultancy selection (not civil works contractors) for Indian government road/highway projects — DPR, Authority's Engineer / Independent Engineer, Supervision, Feasibility, Road Safety.*

---

## 1. Landscape Overview

The road & highways sector is the **most standardised** consultancy-procurement space in Indian government infrastructure. Because MoRTH publishes model RFP documents and NHAI/NHIDCL/state PWDs reuse them almost verbatim, the eligibility rules, evaluation method, and scoring tables are remarkably consistent across authorities and across time (RFPs from 2017 to 2026 share the same skeleton).

Key features that recur in virtually every consultancy RFP examined:

- **Selection method: QCBS (Quality & Cost Based Selection) with an 80:20 technical:financial weight.** This is near-universal for DPR, Authority's Engineer (AE) and Independent Engineer (IE) / Supervision assignments. Multilateral-funded (JICA/World Bank/ADB) NHIDCL tenders also use QCBS 80:20 under the donor's consultant guidelines.
- **Minimum technical qualifying score: 75 out of 100.** A proposal must score ≥75 technical points to have its financial proposal opened.
- **Team Leader gate:** The Team Leader's CV must individually score at least **75%**; if it does not, the *entire* proposal is rejected regardless of overall score. This is the single most important pass/fail rule for a bidder.
- **INFRACON registration is mandatory.** Both the firm and every proposed key personnel must be registered on the MoRTH INFRACON portal (`infracon.nic.in`); bidders form a "Team" and quote a unique INFRACON Team ID with the bid. CVs are auto-validated against INFRACON/Aadhaar/DigiLocker data.
- **Key personnel dominate scoring.** In DPR RFPs, key personnel carry ~50% of technical marks; in AE/IE RFPs, 40–60%. Firm experience and technology capability make up most of the rest; approach & methodology is typically only 5 marks.
- **Turnover floor** is commonly **₹5 crore** average annual consultancy turnover (last 3–5 years), with additional marks for higher turnover.
- **Similar-experience floor** is defined relative to the project's length: aggregate DPR/supervision length ≥ the package length, plus at least one project of 40–50% of the package length, in the last 7 years.

## 2. Key Authorities & Portals

| Authority | Role | Typical consultancy tenders |
|---|---|---|
| **NHAI** (nhai.gov.in) | National highways development/O&M | DPR, Authority's Engineer, Independent Engineer, Supervision, road-inspection technology |
| **MoRTH** (morth.gov.in) | Policy + NH works via State PWDs | Model RFPs; DPR/feasibility for "In-Principle" declared NHs (executed through State PWD NH divisions) |
| **NHIDCL** (nhidcl.com) | NHs in NE/border/hill states, often JICA/ADB/WB-funded | Authority's Engineer, DPR/feasibility (hill roads) |
| **State PWDs / NH Divisions** | Execute MoRTH-funded NH DPRs | DPR, feasibility, pre-construction services |
| **State Road Dev. Corps** (MSRDC, UPEIDA, KRDCL etc.) | Expressways / state highways | DPR, AE, PMC (use MoRTH-style RFPs) |
| **NRIDA / PMGSY** (pmgsy.nic.in) | Rural roads | Third-tier quality monitoring (National Quality Monitors), mandatory Road Safety Audit for PMGSY-III roads >5 km |
| **INFRACON** (infracon.nic.in) | MoRTH portal | Mandatory firm + personnel empanelment / credential validation |
| **CPPP / eProcure** (eprocure.gov.in) | Central e-tender portal | Bid submission platform for most of the above |

## 4. Evaluation Criteria Synthesis

Two dominant scoring archetypes emerge; both total 100 technical marks, both QCBS 80:20, both require ≥75 to pass and impose the Team-Leader-75% gate.

**A. DPR / Feasibility archetype (MoRTH & NHAI DPR):**
- Firm's relevant experience — **25**
- Approach & methodology — **5**
- Materials testing, survey & investigation, equipment/technology (in-house lab, LiDAR, GPR/utility detection, cadastral digitisation) — **20**
- Qualification & competence of key personnel — **50**

**B. Authority's Engineer / Independent Engineer / Supervision archetype (NHAI & NHIDCL):**
- Relevant experience for the assignment — **35** (NHAI O&M/IE) or **20** (NHIDCL/JICA AE)
- Quality of approach & methodology / work plan — **5** (NHAI) or **20** (NHIDCL/JICA)
- Experience in use of technology for road inspection (NSV, FWD, Mobile Bridge Inspection Unit, Retro-Reflectometer) — **20** (relevant to O&M/IE)
- Qualification & competence of key personnel — **40** (NHAI O&M/IE) or **60** (NHIDCL/JICA)

**Cross-cutting rules baked into the framework:**
- **Role weighting of firm experience:** sole firm = 100%, lead JV partner = 75%, other JV partner = 50%, associate = 25%.
- **Length-equivalence factors:** 4-lane(+) length often counted ×1.5 of 2-lane equivalent; Feasibility counted as ⅔ (or ½–⅔) of DPR length; enhancement factors (≈10%/yr) applied to bring older turnover/experience values to present base.
- **CV rules:** below-75% CVs for non-Team-Leader positions are usually tolerated for a limited number (2–3 personnel) and must be replaced before signing; duplicate CV across firms = zero; failure to meet essential *education* = zero for that CV.

## 5. Policy / Model-Document Findings

- **Manual for Procurement of Consultancy & Other Services (MoF, Dept. of Expenditure, updated June 2022)** — the umbrella policy under GFR 2017. Defines the selection systems (QCBS §3.9, LCS §3.8, SSS §3.10, plus QBS/FBS) that sector RFPs implement. Ministries are told to supplement it with their own detailed manuals and standard bidding documents — which is exactly what MoRTH's model RFP does.
- **MoRTH Model RFP (QCBS)** — operationalises the manual for the road sector: TIS + Forms T-2…T-6, Section VII scoring, and the mandatory INFRACON linkage.
- **INFRACON portal** — mandatory credential repository for firms and key personnel across MoRTH/NHAI/NHIDCL/IAHE/state NH agencies; Aadhaar/DigiLocker-linked; enables click-of-a-button technical evaluation and a unique Team ID per bid.
- **NRIDA/PMGSY guidelines** — separate rural-roads quality regime (NQMs + mandatory Road Safety Audit for PMGSY-III >5 km).

## 6. Gaps & Caveats

- **Consultancy fee values are usually not published** in the RFP (the consultant quotes them), so `estimatedValueINR` is null for most entries; the underlying project/civil cost is recorded in the scope summary where the RFP states it.
- Several detailed live-tender PDFs on nhai.gov.in/eprocure are behind session-based downloads; the 2026 NHAI Jind–Gohana IE RFP was accessible and is included as the anchor "live" example, supplemented by recent (2025) NHIDCL RFPs and well-preserved 2017–2019 RFPs on INFRACON, which are the authoritative source for the scoring tables.
- State Road Development Corporations (MSRDC/UPEIDA/KRDCL) reuse MoRTH-style RFPs; a dedicated SRDC tender PDF was not separately captured, but their evaluation logic is covered by the MoRTH model RFP and NHAI DPR/AE entries.
