# Power & Energy Sector — Consultancy Tender Research & Evaluation Framework

_Generated: 2026-07-18. Prepared for the consultancy-bidding webapp (power sector module)._

## 1. Landscape Overview

Consultancy procurement in the Indian power sector is dominated by the **Quality and Cost
Based Selection (QCBS)** method mandated by **Rule 192 of the General Financial Rules (GFR)
2017** and detailed in the **Ministry of Finance "Manual for Procurement of Consultancy &
Other Services" (June 2022)**. Under GFR Rule 192(iv), the technical (non-financial) weight
in QCBS may **never exceed 80%**. In practice the sector clusters around two ratios:

- **QCBS 80:20** — used for complex, quality-critical or multilaterally-funded assignments
  (e.g. World Bank / JICA-funded Owner's Engineer roles at SECI). Minimum qualifying
  technical score is typically **75%**.
- **QCBS 70:30** — the workhorse ratio for domestic PSU consultancy (NHPC design/engineering,
  NTPC PMC, state DISCOM PMC/DPR). Minimum qualifying technical score is typically **70%**.

Two important exceptions to QCBS recur in the power sector:

1. **Empanelment + Least-Cost (L1)** — Used where a pool of pre-vetted consultants is
   maintained and individual assignments are then awarded to the lowest bidder among the
   empaneled/technically-qualified firms. Seen in **PFC Consulting Ltd (PFCCL)** transmission
   survey/DPR bids (empanelment by "Category"/"Area Code") and **RECPDCL** solar PMC
   empanelment.
2. **Two-stage RFQ→RFP** — Used for large transaction-advisory / privatisation mandates
   (e.g. DISCOM disinvestment), where a Request for Qualification shortlists bidders before a
   QCBS RFP.

The technical score in a power-sector QCBS is almost always split across three heads:
**(a) Firm's relevant experience**, **(b) Approach & Methodology**, and **(c) Key Personnel
/ Team**. The relative weight of (a) vs (c) varies sharply by assignment type — engineering /
OE assignments load experience (SECI: firm 45 / staff 40), while advisory / PMU assignments
load personnel (REC: firm 25 / experts 75).

## 2. Key Authorities & Their Roles

| Authority | Role in power consultancy procurement |
|---|---|
| **Ministry of Power (MoP) / CEA** | Policy, scheme design (RDSS), technical standards, concurrent quality monitoring |
| **PFC & REC** (Nodal Agencies for RDSS) | Issue Standard Bidding Documents (SBD) for PMA & TPQMA; hire own advisory consultants; appoint Third-Party Quality Monitoring Agencies |
| **PFC Consulting Ltd (PFCCL)** / RECPDCL / RECTPCL | Bid-process coordinators & consultancy arms; run transmission survey/DPR and solar PMC empanelments |
| **POWERGRID (PGCIL)** | Transmission utility; publishes RfE/RfP for land scheduling, survey, execution agencies |
| **NTPC / NHPC / SJVN** | Generation PSUs; hire Owner's Engineer / PMC / design consultants and also sell consultancy |
| **SECI** (under MNRE) | Solar/renewables + BESS; Owner's Engineer selection under World Bank QCBS |
| **MNRE** | PM-KUSUM, rooftop solar programmes; DISCOMs act as implementing/tendering agencies |
| **State DISCOMs / TRANSCOs / GENCOs** (MSEDCL, UPPCL, TANGEDCO etc.) | PMC, DPR, feeder-separation, asset-register and scheme-implementation consultancy |

### 3.1 SECI — Owner's Engineer, 100 MW Solar + 40 MW/120 MWh BESS, Rajnandgaon (Chhattisgarh)
- Ref: `SECI/C&P/RfP/2020/CG/100/120/OE` (World Bank STEP ref IN-SECI-214410-CS-QCBS).
- Method: **QCBS 80:20**, World Bank Procurement Regulations, min technical score **75%**.
- Technical marks (out of 100): **Specific experience of the firm = 45** (graded by cumulative

### 3.10 Policy — MoF "Manual for Procurement of Consultancy & Other Services" (June 2022)
- Governs all central-government consultancy procurement. Chapter 3 lays out selection systems:
  LCS (§3.8), **QCBS (§3.9)**, Single Source (§3.10), Fixed Budget Selection (§3.11). Chapter 6
  details the RfP process, the Consultancy Evaluation Committee (CEC), responsiveness screening,
  technical (quality) evaluation, cost evaluation and combined scoring. Reinforces the GFR-2017
  cap of **≤80% technical weight**.
- Source type: policy-document.

### 3.11 Policy — RDSS Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme Guidelines (PMA)
- Component-III (Project Management): each DISCOM appoints one or more **Project Management
  Agencies (PMA)** for plan/DPR preparation, tendering, monitoring, quality assurance, material
  inspection and results evaluation (excluding signing Joint Measurement Certificates). PMA
  charges capped at **2.5% of Component-I (metering) GBS and 1.5% of Component-II (distribution
  infrastructure) project cost**. New PMAs (for bid-process/monitoring works) must be appointed
  per the Nodal Agency SBD. Separately, the scheme funds concurrent/third-party quality
  monitoring (TPQMA).
- Source type: policy-document.

## 4. Evaluation-Criteria Synthesis

Consolidating across the tenders above, a consultant bidding on a power-sector assignment is
typically scored on:

| Criterion | Typical weight | Range observed | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Firm's relevant power-sector experience | ~30% | 25% (advisory) – 45% (OE/engineering) | Graded by number & value of similar assignments, often proportional to highest awarded value |
| Key personnel / team | ~40% | 40% (OE) – 75% (advisory/PMU) | Per-expert marks for qualification + years beyond a minimum; interviews common; payroll penalties |
| Approach, methodology & work plan | ~15% | 10% – 20% | Sometimes assessed via presentation |
| Financial capacity (turnover / net worth) | Gate (0–15% scored) | Pass/fail gate in most; scored (up to 15 marks) in transaction advisory | Holding-company fallback common with LoU |

**Dominant method: QCBS (70:30 domestic, 80:20 multilateral-funded); typical minimum
qualifying technical score 70–75/100.** Empanelment+L1 applies to transmission survey/DPR and
solar PMC pools; two-stage RFQ→RFP applies to large advisory/privatisation mandates.

## 5. Gaps & Caveats

- **Live full-RFP PDFs are frequently gated** behind GeM/e-procurement logins; several entries
  therefore rely on official NIT/detail pages, authority "what's-new" notices, archived RFPs, or
  the scheme/policy documents. `sourceType` is marked honestly per entry.
- **Exact estimated values are not always public** (SECI OE, NTPC OE, MSEDCL PMC TOR); these are
  left `null` rather than guessed.
- **POWERGRID (PGCIL)** publishes many transmission empanelment/execution notices but few pure
  consultancy QCBS RFPs were openly downloadable at research time; PFCCL/RECPDCL cover the
  transmission and solar consultancy space instead.
- The MSEDCL MTSKPY PMC entry exposes only a TOR at its public URL; its detailed marks live in
  the full RFP and are not asserted here.
