# Urban & Smart Cities — Consultancy Procurement Analysis

**Sector:** `urban` — Urban & Smart Cities
**Prepared:** 2026-07-18
**Scope:** Consultancy (not works/contractor) tenders for urban infrastructure — smart cities, urban roads/streetscaping, municipal solid waste, affordable housing (PMAY-U), urban transport terminals, riverfront/redevelopment, city master plans and GIS-based planning. Water-supply/sewerage-specific and metro-rail tenders are deliberately excluded (covered by other sector agents).

---

## 1. Landscape Overview

Urban infrastructure consultancy in India is procured by a fragmented but well-patterned ecosystem: central-mission SPVs (Smart City companies), municipal corporations and urban local bodies (ULBs), state urban development authorities/improvement trusts, and state infrastructure/finance corporations acting as procuring agents. Despite the variety of issuers, the **procurement mechanics are strikingly uniform**, because almost everyone anchors to the same two policy instruments: the Ministry of Finance *Manual for Procurement of Consultancy & Other Services (2022)* and the MoHUA/MoUD *Smart City Mission Guidelines* and model RFP toolkit.

Key observed patterns:

- **QCBS is the default** selection method, with **80:20 (technical:financial)** the most common split for planning, PMC and design/advisory work. A **70:30** split appears on smart-city assignments that lean toward IT/system-integration (e.g. ICCC, master system integrators).
- **Least Cost Selection (LCS/L1)** is a real and recurring minority method — seen here on the Uttarakhand/Roorkee GIS master plan (AMRUT) and the Rajasthan PMAY-U SLTC/CLTC manpower contract — used where scope is standardized and the state wants price to decide among technically-qualified firms.
- **Two-envelope, gated process everywhere:** a pass/fail pre-qualification/eligibility screen (legal status, turnover, similar experience, non-blacklisting, EMD), then a scored technical evaluation out of 100; financial envelopes open only for firms clearing the minimum technical score.
- **Minimum technical score clusters at 70-75**, ranging from 50-60 (solid-waste / IEC / manpower assignments) up to 80 (GIS master-plan formulation).
- **Key personnel dominate technical scoring** (typically 35-55 of 100 marks), followed by firm experience (20-40), methodology/presentation (10-30) and — in a subset of tenders — turnover scored directly (5-30).

## 3. Tender-by-Tender Notes

1. **Chandigarh Smart City PMC (RFP 1/2017)** — QCBS 80:20, min tech **75**. Very high turnover bar (**₹500 Cr** single / ₹250 Cr per JV member). Classic MoHUA PMC structure: firm experience 40 / methodology 20 / key personnel 40. Hosted on SmartNet NIUA. *Archived.*

2. **Puducherry Smart City PMC (Feb 2018)** — QCBS 80:20, min tech **70**. Turnover **₹30 Cr** single / ₹10 Cr per member; EMD ₹20 L. Firm experience is scored per sub-sector (urban infra, mobility, ICT/e-gov, affordable housing/slum, energy) at ~₹100 lakh/assignment thresholds; firm exp 40 / methodology 10 / personnel 50. *Archived.*

3. **NDMC Smart City PMC (RFP 01/NDMCSCL/PMC/2016)** — QCBS, min tech **70**, EMD ₹25 L. Distinctive: **financial strength is directly scored** (10 marks on a turnover slab starting ₹250 Cr), plus bid-advisory experience on SCP-listed cities (5 marks each) and a detailed key-personnel table (Project Director 8, etc.). 19-member team, 24-month engagement. *Archived.*

4. **Nashik Smart City PMC (RFP 01/2017-18/NMSCDCL)** — QCBS 80:20 (time-based, performance-linked), min tech **70**. Per-position scoring split **20% general qualifications / 80% adequacy & relevant experience**. *Archived.*

5. **Bhavnagar (BMC) ICCC PMC (BMC/COMPUTER/01/2025-26)** — QCBS 80:20, min tech **70**. Scope: PMC for Integrated Command & Control Centre, CCTV, OFC network, IT-enabled infra. Pre-qualification gate + technical presentation-cum-demonstration. Municipal Commissioner may lower the 70 threshold if no bidder qualifies. *Live (2025-26).*

6. **VMRDA Vizag Bay City Area Development Plan** — QCBS, min tech **70**, EMD ₹10 L. Planning/design-review assignment: GIS base map, SWOC, land-pooling/PPP framework, disaster-resilience and smart/digital infra planning. *Archived.*

7. **Ranchi (RMC) Transaction Advisory + PMC — Nagababa Khatal PPP** — QCBS 80:20 (composite = 0.8T + 0.2F). Project cost ~**₹100 Cr**; turnover **₹10 Cr**. Eligibility requires 2+ PPP transaction-advisory assignments AND 2+ PMC civil-infra assignments, each >₹20 Cr. Project experience scored 40 (5 marks/assignment, bonus for Jharkhand). Good archetype for PPP transaction advisory. *Archived.*

8. **OUIDF / Berhampur MSW Independent Engineer (RFP 05/OUIDF-178/2017)** — QCBS, min tech **75**, KfW-assisted. Supervision/IE role on a DBOT solid-waste project. Modest bars: turnover **₹3 Cr**, one similar assignment ≥₹25 lakh fee. Methodology/presentation block detailed at 30 marks. *Archived.*

9. **Arunachal Pradesh SBM 2.0-U State PMU (RFP_01_2022_23)** — QCBS, min tech **60**, EMD ₹5 L. PMU across 47 ULBs for solid & liquid waste. Turnover bar surprisingly high (**₹50 Cr**). Team experience dominates (60 marks, scored by expert years-of-experience bands); firm experience 10; methodology/presentation 30. *Archived.*

10. **Uttarakhand / Roorkee GIS Master Plan (AMRUT)** — **Least Cost Selection (LCS)**, min tech **80**. Firm experience 20 / methodology 30 / key personnel 50 (Team Leader-Urban Planner 15, GIS Expert 15, Transport Planner 10, Socio-economic 10). Illustrates the LCS-after-technical-gate model for standardized planning. *Archived.*

11. **HSVP Sector 29 Gurugram Master Plan (Revised 01.07.2024)** — QCBS, min tech **75**, turnover **₹50 Cr/yr**. Master-planner-cum-engineering firm; drone survey + GIS + urban design + integrated infrastructure + preliminary engineering. Turnover scored on a slab (₹50 Cr = 5, ₹51-100 Cr = 7, up to 10). *Archived/recent.*

12. **Ujjain Smart City empanelment (NIT USCL/427, May 2026)** — QCBS empanelment of consultants/architects for building & civic infra (technical consultancy, architectural planning, DPR, bid process management). Two groups by project size (≤₹10 Cr / >₹10 Cr). Low entry bar: consultancy turnover **₹0.25 Cr**; experience of 3 projects >₹2 Cr (or 2>₹2.5 Cr or 1>₹4 Cr). UDIN certification required. Good small/mid-firm design-consultancy archetype. *Live (2026).*

13. **RUDSICO PMAY-U 2.0 SLTC/CLTC (Feb 2026)** — **Least Cost Selection (L1)**, min tech **60**. Tender value **₹2631.76 Lakh (~₹26.32 Cr)**. Turnover ≥30% of estimated value (~₹8.69 Cr), bid security 2% of value. Technical 100 = financial strength 30 / previous experience 30 / methodology 10 / key staff 30 (7 SLTC + 5 CLTC positions, ~40% qualification / 60% experience per expert). Housing-mission manpower/PMU archetype. *Live (2026).*

## 4. Evaluation Criteria Synthesis

Consolidating the 13 tenders, urban consultant technical evaluation resolves into four repeatable blocks (marks vary but the structure is consistent):

| Criterion | Typical weight | How it's scored |
|---|---|---|
| **Key personnel (Team Leader + experts)** | 35-55 | Per-position marks on education + relevant years of experience; Team Leader CV often must independently clear ~75%; below-minimum CVs may score zero and can fail the whole proposal. |
| **Firm's relevant experience** | 20-40 | Fixed marks per qualifying assignment above a value threshold (~₹20 Cr project cost or ~₹1 Cr fee); bonus for same sub-sector / same state. |
| **Approach, methodology, work plan & presentation** | 10-30 | TOR-responsiveness, realism of work plan, staffing balance; Team-Leader-led presentation is expected and frequently scored. |
| **Financial strength (turnover)** | 0-30 | Usually a pass/fail eligibility gate; directly scored on a slab in a subset (NDMC 10, HSVP 10, RUDSICO 30, PMAY cells). |

**Eligibility gate patterns.** Turnover thresholds span three orders of magnitude by assignment size: ₹0.25 Cr (small design empanelment) → ₹3-10 Cr (MSW IE, PPP advisory) → ₹30-50 Cr (mid smart-city / master plan / PMU) → ₹250-500 Cr (large smart-city PMCs). Several tenders express the bar as a **percentage of estimated value** (e.g. RUDSICO 30%, some DPR RFPs 50%). Non-blacklisting affidavits, CA/UDIN-certified financials, and an Indian office are near-universal.

**Method distribution in this dataset:** QCBS 80:20 (Chandigarh, Puducherry, Nashik, Bhavnagar, Ranchi, plus HSVP as QCBS), QCBS unspecified/general (NDMC, VMRDA, Arunachal, OUIDF, Ujjain), LCS (Roorkee, RUDSICO). **QCBS 80:20 is the dominant method.**

**Minimum technical score distribution:** 60 (Arunachal SBM, RUDSICO), 70 (Puducherry, NDMC, Nashik, Bhavnagar, VMRDA), 75 (Chandigarh, OUIDF, HSVP), 80 (Roorkee). Typical/median ≈ **75** (with 70 the single most common value).

## 5. Policy Document Findings

- **MoF Manual for Procurement of Consultancy & Other Services (2022).** The governing document under GFR 2017. It codifies the selection menu that urban tenders draw from — **QCBS (Sec 3.9)**, Least Cost Selection (Sec 3.8), Single Source Selection (3.10), Fixed Budget (3.11) — and the two-stage, minimum-technical-score, sealed-financial-envelope process. It explains why the same evaluation skeleton recurs across otherwise unrelated ULBs and SPVs. (Note: a 2023-updated variant is also in circulation on some PSU sites, but the June-2022 MoF edition remains the reference.)

- **MoF Model Tender Document for Consultancy Services (CPPP).** Provides the standard REoI → RFP flow (EOI shortlisting followed by RFP to shortlisted firms) and the qualification/evaluation forms that state and municipal RFPs visibly copy.

- **Smart City Mission Guidelines (MoUD/MoHUA).** Establishes the SPV-led delivery model and, critically, states that **MoUD technically empanels a panel of consulting firms** from which States/SPVs may draw, requesting financial proposals via the **template RFP in the Mission Toolkit**. It authorizes SPVs to appoint PMCs for designing, developing, managing and implementing area-based projects, and defines the SCP evaluation criteria that shape PMC scope. This is the origin of the recurring "firm experience 40 / methodology 10-20 / key personnel 40-50" PMC scoring template seen in Chandigarh, Puducherry, Nashik and NDMC.

## 6. Gaps & Caveats

- **Consultancy fee values are usually not disclosed** in these RFPs (fees are what bidders quote in the financial envelope). Where a figure exists it is the *project/works cost* (Ranchi ~₹100 Cr) or a *tender/estimated value* (RUDSICO ₹26.32 Cr); `estimatedValueINR` is left `null` elsewhere with an explanatory label rather than fabricated.
- **Some tender reference numbers and publication dates are not printed** in the extracted documents (VMRDA, Ranchi, Roorkee, HSVP) and are recorded as `null`.
- **Two live-tender PDFs sit behind aggregators/hosts** (RUDSICO via Scribd; several TN bus-terminal transaction-advisory RFPs on sigmatenders/GeM were found but not deep-verified) — only firmly-verifiable documents were included.
- **Riverfront/lakefront and BRTS-specific consultancy tenders** were searched but strong, verifiable consultant RFPs were sparser than PMC/master-plan/SWM ones; the PPP transaction-advisory archetype (Ranchi) and area-development plan (VMRDA) cover the redevelopment/urban-transport advisory space instead.
- Some borderline documents (NHAI "direct connectivity in Indian cities" DPR; NCRPB functional plan) were **deliberately excluded** to avoid overlap with the road and water sector agents.
