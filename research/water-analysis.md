# Water & Sanitation Sector — Consultancy Tender Research & Evaluation Framework

**Sector:** `water` — Water & Sanitation
**Prepared:** 2026-07-18
**Purpose:** Feed the water-sector module of a webapp that helps consultancy firms self-assess against Indian government water/sewerage consultancy tenders (DPR, PMC/PDMC, supervision, third-party inspection, feasibility, transaction advisory).

---

## 1. Landscape Overview

India's water & sanitation capital programme is enormous and consultancy-intensive. Three national programmes drive most consultancy demand:

- **Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM)** — rural piped water supply ("Har Ghar Jal"), implemented by state PHEDs / Jal Nigams. Generates a very large volume of **DPR-preparation** and **third-party inspection / PMC** tenders, often at district/scheme level and relatively small in value.
- **AMRUT 2.0** (MoHUA) — urban water supply, sewerage/septage and water-body rejuvenation. Its signature consultancy vehicle is the **State-level Project Development & Management Consultant (PDMC)**, procured by state urban development agencies (SUDA/GSUDA/LSGD etc.).
- **Namami Gange / NMCG** — river rejuvenation via sewerage infrastructure, increasingly on **Hybrid Annuity (HAM) PPP** mode, requiring **PMC**, **Owner's/Independent Engineer** and **transaction advisory** consultants.

Alongside these, large urban utilities (Delhi Jal Board, BWSSB, GWSSB, Maharashtra Jeevan Pradhikaran, MP Jal Nigam) run their own high-value consultancy procurements — master planning, NRW reduction, DPR, DSC/PMC, and independent quality inspection.

**Consultant selection is overwhelmingly by QCBS.** The legal/procedural backbone is the **GFR 2017 (Rules 192/193)** and the **MoF Manual for Procurement of Consultancy & Other Services, 2022**, which set out QCBS, LCS, QBS, FBS and SSS. GFR caps the technical (non-financial) weight at **80%**, which is exactly why the highest ratio observed in practice is **80:20**, while **70:30** is the everyday default for AMRUT/JJM PDMC and DPR work.

## 3. Tender-by-Tender Notes (11 tenders)

### water-001 — Delhi Jal Board: General Consultancy (Water Master Plan / NRW) — *live-tender*
- **Method:** QCBS **80:20**; **min technical 70%**.
- **Turnover gate:** ≥ **Rs 75 Cr** avg (last 3 FYs), UDIN-certified.
- **Eligibility:** "Eligible assignments" serving ≥ 1 million population, ~80% complete in last 10 years, across NRW study, urban WS distribution dev/rehab, transaction advisory/BPM, and 24×7 master planning.
- **Technical scoring (100):** six firm-experience items @5 each (30), turnover (5), approach & methodology incl. presentation (20), **key experts (45)** — Team Leader cum PM Expert = 9, Deputy TL = 6, 6 supervision engineers = 6, plus specialists. This is the most detailed, highest-bar RFP in the sample and typifies large-utility practice.

### water-002 — Kerala LSGD/Suchitwa: AMRUT 2.0 PDMC — *model-rfp*
- **Method:** QCBS **70:30** (CQCCBS); **min technical 60**.
- **Turnover gate:** ≥ **Rs 25 Cr** avg; positive net worth; firm ≥ 10 yrs old; **100 personnel** on payroll.
- **Experience gate:** 3 urban water/wastewater PDMC/PMU assignments (fee > Rs 5 Cr) in last 7 yrs.
- **Technical scoring (100):** years in existence (5), turnover (10), large PMU experience (10), water/liquid-waste experience (15), **key personnel (25)**, **methodology (25)**, NGO/academic partnering (10). Excellent template for AMRUT PDMC scoring.

### water-003 — GWSSB: Independent Review & Quality Inspection Agency — *live-tender*
- **Method:** QCBS; **min technical 70**; **value ~Rs 6.8 Cr**.
- **Turnover gate:** ≥ **Rs 100 Cr** avg; **200 personnel** on payroll/empanelled.
- **Technical scoring:** turnover (10; banded to >300 Cr = 10), similar state/UT WS assignments (20), completed project ≥ Rs 1000 Cr (15), personnel strength (10), plus 9 key personnel + methodology. Represents the **third-party inspection / IVA / QC** consultancy category.

### water-004 — NMCG: PMC (Institutional Support) — *live-tender*
- **Method:** QCBS.
- **Turnover gate:** ≥ **Rs 34 Cr** avg, of which ≥ **Rs 17 Cr** from PMC/programme-management consultancy.
- **Experience gate:** ≥ 7 govt-sector consultancy assignments in 7 yrs; ≥ 2 similar PMC assignments (fee ≥ Rs 2 Cr each). Full marks matrix in the RFP; eligibility thresholds captured. Good example of a **central-mission PMC**.

### water-005 — MP Jal Nigam: PMC for 9 Multi-Village Rural WS Schemes (NIT-34, NDB) — *archived-tender*
- **Method:** **LCS** (Least Cost) after technical qualification; **min technical 800/1000 (80%)**.
- **Turnover gate:** ≥ **Rs 35 Cr** avg.
- **Experience gate:** PMC/SQC/DSC on ≥ 1 WS scheme > Rs 500 Cr OR 3 schemes > Rs 200 Cr each since 2007.
- **Scoring (1000):** turnover (50; <35 Cr disqualified, >75 Cr = 50), firm experience, **Team Leader 150**, **Deputy TL 150**. Useful counter-example showing **LCS** and a 1000-point scale with dominant CV weighting.

### water-006 — PHED Morena (MP): JJM Piped WS DPR preparation — *live-tender*
- Ref **NIT16EEPHE26-27MORENA13.4.2026**; **value Rs 1.22 Cr**; 120-day DPR job.
- Procured as a **"Works"-category** two-cover (technical + BOQ) tender, i.e. **item-rate/BOQ** rather than QCBS. Illustrates that small **JJM DPR** work is often let as works, not classic consultancy QCBS — an important nuance for firms bidding at scheme level.

### water-007 — GSUDA (Goa): AMRUT 2.0 PDMC — *live-tender*
- State-wide PDMC for water/wastewater/Swachh Bharat; **QCBS** selection. Requires prior national water-mission/consultancy experience. Marks matrix not extracted at source; mirrors the Kerala PDMC pattern.

### water-008 — SUDA (West Bengal): AMRUT urban water consultancy / knowledge partner — *live-tender*
- **Method:** QCBS **70:30** (CQCBS); **min qualifying 70%**.
- **Turnover gate:** ≥ **Rs 20 Cr** avg; 2 similar completed projects in last 3 calendar years.
- **Key personnel:** Team Leader/Strategy Expert, Deputy TL, PPP Specialist, RFP Specialist, Water Quality Analyst, Legal Advisor — a more **advisory/PPP-structuring** flavoured consultancy. Turnover scored on a sliding scale (top band ~≥ Rs 60 Cr).

### water-009 — PMRDA: PMC for Pawana River Pollution Abatement — *live-tender*
- **Method:** QCBS; **min technical 60**.
- Scope: review sanctioned DPR, prepare tender docs & BPM, act as PMC + technical auditor, and supervise O&M up to **15 years**. Requires licensed design software (WaterGems/Bentley, ESRI, Autodesk, Sewer-CAD/Gem, GIS, BIM). Represents **river/sewerage PMC** at metropolitan-authority level.

### water-010 — BWSSB: Cauvery VI Stage DPR consultancy — *archived-tender*
- **Value Rs 95 lakh**; scope includes study, **EIA**, alignment and cost estimation for a bulk-water DPR. Typically QCBS. Sourced from a reputable news report (The Hindu) confirming the tender's existence, value and scope; full RFP scoring not available. Good example of a mid-size **DPR** consultancy at a major utility.

### water-011 — NMCG / UP Jal Nigam: Project Engineer (Independent Engineer), STPs (HAM PPP) — *archived-tender*
- Ref **Pc-11011/3/2020-NMCG** (issued 23 Jul 2020), Moradabad/Budhana/Muzaffarnagar; **15-yr O&M** oversight under **Hybrid Annuity PPP**.
- **Financial gate:** ≥ **Rs 5 Cr/annum** professional-fee revenue in each of last 3 FYs.
- **Experience gate:** 2–3 eligible sewerage/STP assignments (≥ 25 MLD) in last 10 yrs. Represents the **Independent/Owner's Engineer** consultancy under PPP.

## 4. Evaluation Criteria Synthesis

Across all 11 tenders, the technical score is built from **four consistent families**, plus turnover which is mainly a hard gate:

1. **Firm's relevant water-sector experience (typ. 20–45%)** — counts and values of similar DPR/PMC/supervision/inspection assignments, usually last 5–10 years. Almost always scored on **step scales** (1 project → 2 → 3+) with **value/scale bands** (> Rs 200 Cr / 500 Cr / 1000 Cr; population ≥ 1 million).
2. **Financial strength / turnover (typ. 5–10 marks, but a hard eligibility gate)** — avg annual turnover last 3 FYs, banded (e.g. Kerala 25–50/50–75/>75 Cr → 3/7/10). Must be CA/UDIN-certified.
3. **Firm strength & standing (typ. 5–15 marks)** — years in existence and **qualified personnel on payroll** (100 gate for PDMC, 200 for inspection; banded 200–500/500–1000/>1000 → 2/5/10).
4. **Key personnel CVs by role (typ. 25–45%)** — the largest lever after firm experience. Scored per role on **qualification + years of experience** (+ regional/role-specific experience). **Team Leader is the single most-weighted CV** (9/100 at DJB; 150/1000 at MPJNM). Team Leader/Deputy shortfalls can render a bid non-responsive.
5. **Approach, methodology & work plan (typ. 20–25%)** — TOR understanding, project-specific methodology, work plan/timeframe, resource deployment, and often a **presentation** to the committee.

**Minimum technical score:** 60% for AMRUT/JJM PDMC (Kerala 60, WB 70, PMRDA 60); 70% for large-utility (DJB 70, GWSSB 70); up to 80% under LCS (MPJNM 800/1000).

**Financial evaluation:** invariably `Sf = 100 × Fm / F` (lowest priced = 100), then combined `S = St×T% + Sf×(1−T%)`. Several RFPs (e.g. Kerala) also disqualify **abnormally low bids** (< 70% of the average of the lowest three, excluding L1).

**Practical takeaway for a bidding firm:** eligibility (turnover + 1–2 qualifying similar assignments + personnel count) is a gate, not a differentiator. Competitive scores come from (a) **depth of directly comparable water references** with the right scale/value/population, and (b) **fielding strong named experts**, especially a heavyweight Team Leader, plus a genuinely project-specific methodology.

## 5. Policy Document Findings

- **MoF Manual for Procurement of Consultancy & Other Services, 2022** — codifies the selection systems (LCS §3.8, QCBS §3.9, SSS, FBS) and the two-stage evaluation (responsiveness → quality → cost), the Consultancy Evaluation Committee, and the QCBS combined-score mechanics. This is the master reference behind every water QCBS RFP.
- **GFR Rule 192 (QCBS) & 193 (LCS)** — QCBS is mandated for quality-sensitive consultancy; the RFP must publish the minimum qualifying technical score and the technical:financial weights (70:30, 60:40, 50:50 etc.), and the **technical weight must not exceed 80%**. LCS is reserved for standard/routine assignments (audits, non-complex design) — consistent with MPJNM using LCS for straightforward multi-village supervision.
- **AMRUT 2.0 Operational Guidelines (Oct 2021)** — establishes the **PDMC** construct and the City Water Balance Plan / City Water Action Plan process, with a 24×7 water-supply and reuse orientation that shapes urban DPR/PDMC scopes.
- **JJM Operational Guidelines (DDWS)** — set the rural piped-water context; state PHEDs/Jal Nigams procure DPR and third-party inspection consultants under this programme, frequently as works-category tenders at scheme level (see water-006).
- **CPPP Standard RFP for Consultancy Services (eProcure)** — the model RFP/REoI that state agencies adapt; confirms that **key-expert competence is evaluated at RFP (not shortlisting) stage** and that eligibility is pass/fail.
- **CPHEEO Manuals** (referenced, not individually tendered) — the technical design standards for water supply and sewerage that all DPRs must comply with; relevant to DPR-preparation scope quality.

## 6. Gaps & Caveats

- **Estimated values** are frequently not published in the RFP body (especially for multi-year PMC/PDMC assignments); left `null` where not verifiable rather than estimated.
- **Exact marks matrices** could not be fully extracted for water-004 (NMCG PMC), water-007 (GSUDA), water-010 (BWSSB DPR) and water-011 (NMCG Project Engineer) — eligibility thresholds are captured, but some `maxMarks` are `null` to avoid fabrication.
- **water-006** (PHED Morena) and **water-010** (BWSSB) are DPR procurements where the primary accessible source was a tender-aggregator/news report; core facts (ref, value, scope, dates) are from those sources and marked accordingly.
- Some source URLs are **document mirrors/aggregators** (e.g. gtai.de for the DJB RFP, tenderkart for GWSSB, docslib for the NMCG PPP RFP, samsstc for the NMCG PMC) rather than the issuing authority's own portal, because live authority PDFs were not always directly retrievable; the underlying RFPs and refs are genuine.
- **NWDA and WAPCOS** were investigated as authorities but no distinct, verifiable consultant-selection RFP with an extractable scoring matrix was secured within scope; WAPCOS is primarily a bidder (govt consultancy PSU) rather than a tenderer of consultancy.
- The `estimatedValueLabel` uses descriptive text where a numeric value is unavailable, so the app should treat `estimatedValueINR: null` as "not disclosed".
